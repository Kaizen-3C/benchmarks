### Bounded per-lib cost

KD's spend is bounded by `n_files × max_attempts × per_file_token_budget`. The most expensive single KD-S lib was chardet at $5.15. The cheapest was deprecated at $0.22. **Range: 23×.**

OH's spend is unbounded. Most expensive single OH-Sonnet lib: pyjwt at $31.00 — and **it didn't resolve**. The cheapest OH-GPT lib: jinja at $0.74. **Range: ~42× with a tail of unbounded failures.**

### 100% library coverage at modest cost

KD-Sonnet completed all 16 libs for $21.95 in 4.5 hours. KD-GPT-5.4 completed all 16 for $10.57 in 3.2 hours. **OH-Sonnet covered only 6 of 16 for $94.31** because the unbounded per-lib cost made full coverage prohibitive at our budget. **OH-GPT-5.4 covered 14 of 16** at $30.38.

## What we learned

### 1. Caching is decisive — and it's the gap between OH's published numbers and what local-Docker users see

KD's 92% cache hit rate (Anthropic ephemeral) vs OH's 0% (no cache_control headers) explains a 9.8× cost gap on the libs both architectures touched. OpenHands' published $1.88/lib for Sonnet 4.6 is achievable only with caching infrastructure they don't expose to local-Docker users — as documented in [ADR-0060 Amendment 2](../../.architecture/decisions/ADR-0060-commit0-greenfield-benchmark.md).

**Action:** Caching is non-negotiable for any architectural comparison. Future OH-equivalent runs MUST use a litellm-proxy that injects cache_control on the conversation history.

### 2. Each architecture's weakness IS the other's strength

| Weakness | Architecture | Symptom | What the other does instead |
|---|---|---|---|
| No test-side visibility | KD | voluptuous, marshmallow, jinja stuck at 0% / collection broken | OH reads `tests/*.py` imports, ensures package exposes them |
| Unbounded cost on hard libs | OH | pyjwt-Sonnet $31 unresolved | KD bounded at $0.45 for pyjwt, got 45% partial |
| Per-file regen damages working code | KD | pyjwt -28pp, simpy -29pp, wcwidth -3pp regressions | OH's tool-use can verify before commit |
| 0% cache hit rate | OH | 9.8× cost overhead | KD wraps every spec-block in cache_control |

This is the design hint for v2 — the architectures should compose. ADR-0063 (round-trip) is the next benchmark genre that will test composed architectures.

### 3. Reflection-without-grounding is model-dependent

B3 Reflexion regressed Sonnet by **−18.9 pp** but improved GPT-5.4 by **+5.0 pp** on the same task class. The cdaor-benchmarks §5.8a "Reflexion universally beats CD-AOR" claim is empirically refuted on multi-file Greenfield. **Future Reflexion citations must name the model.**

### 4. B2 GPT-5.4 single-shot is the cheap-quality frontier

At **$0.0038 per passing test** ($2.88 for 767 passes), single-shot GPT-5.4 is the cheapest baseline per unit of pass-rate. Any agent architecture that costs more than $0.004/test must justify the marginal spend.

### 5. Five floor libs = five distinct architectural failure modes

| Floor lib | Why it's hard | Who cracked it |
|---|---|---|
| chardet | massive data tables (376 tests) needing precise probabilistic mappings | **KD-S** (4.3%) — per-file regen on the data files |
| babel | locale data + 22 collection errors | **KD-S** (3.7% + collection unlock) |
| marshmallow | `utils.timestamp` cross-file dependency breaks imports | **OH-G** (RESOLVED) — read tests, fixed exports |
| jinja | f-string SyntaxError + cross-import | **OH-G** (RESOLVED) — Sonnet hit max-iter on this one |
| minitorch | tensor library with 10 collection errors; complex | **none cracked it** — open architectural problem |

**No single architecture cracks all 5.** The benchmark is a useful filter for "is your architecture monolithic enough to fail at multiple failure modes?" Most are; ours and OH's are.

### 6. Data is reproducible to the public

Every result JSON, every script, every aggregate, every per-lib breakdown is committed to `benchmarks/commit0/results/` and `benchmarks/commit0/baselines/`. Pinned versions: `commit0==0.1.8`, `openhands-sdk==1.16.1`, dataset SHA `944c325cf...`, Sonnet 4.6, GPT-5.4. The CAMPAIGN_README documents the WSL2 + Docker setup to re-run from scratch.

## Closing

The campaign produced what it was designed to produce: a reproducible architectural-weakness fingerprint for two distinct architectures (OH agent-loop, KD per-file decompose) across two providers (Sonnet 4.6, GPT-5.4) on a 16-library benchmark. The fingerprint identifies four concrete fixes (test-import visibility, per-test identity tracking, prompt caching, agent termination heuristic) that are all single-architecture changes, all cheap to implement, all directly addressed by the data we now have.
